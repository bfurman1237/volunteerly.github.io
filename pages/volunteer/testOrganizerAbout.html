Role Overview

As a Story Teller, you will read books aloud to children ages 5 and under at the Quenjo Municipal Library. This is a warm, engaging role perfect for volunteers who enjoy working with young learners and encouraging early reading habits.

Responsibilities

  • Read children’s books aloud in a lively and engaging manner.
  • Encourage children to participate through questions and interaction.
  • Assist with selecting age-appropriate books, if comfortable.
  • Help maintain a clean and welcoming reading environment.
  • Represent the library with warmth, patience, and professionalism.

Hours & Commitment

2 hours per week Scheduling is flexible, and the library team will work with your availability. Volunteers are encouraged to commit for at least one month.

Who This Role Is Great For

  • Anyone who enjoys reading and storytelling.
  • People comfortable around young children.
  • Introverts or extroverts — both thrive here!
  • Volunteers looking for a low-stress, meaningful role.

About the Organization

The Quenjo Municipal Library serves thousands of families each year with a passion for literacy, learning, and community connection. The Story Time program is one of their most cherished offerings, helping children build early cognitive and language skills through shared storytelling experiences.
